I was 7 years old at the time.

I'm not sure of all the details.

Something had happened in the house, and all evidence pointed to me as the culprit.

But for once, I was completely innocent. 100% non-guilty. I protested, then explained and even begged. Yet it
was all to no avail.

Not only did I get punished, now the punishment was made worse because of "lying".

Shamefaced, grounded and sent to my bedroom I was boiling over. I've never been as bitter as I was at that
moment. As soon as my bedroom door closed, tears of anguish and bitterness streamed down my face. I sobbed.
"Life isn't fair."

Maybe I'm the only one who did this, but I have a sneaky feeling that some of you reading this were (or "are")
the same. I started to think thoughts like this:

- "If I wasn't here then they'd notice."
- "I'm definitely running away!"
- "If I stopped breathing and died right now, then they'd be sorry."

After about 30 seconds of holding my breath I realised that it wasn't such an easy thing to do. So now
breathless(!), sobbing, teary eyed and utterly helpless I cried into my pillow. Life was futile.

I'm not sure how long I was in this state for. Probably hours. The daylight that had streamed through my
window had now been replaced with a soft ethereal moonlit beam. It was evening, and I felt forgotten by the
world.

My self-pity and bitterness was quietly interrupted by a soft knock on the bedroom door. It creaked open. My
dad's head peered through and he gently asked, "Ifiok can we speak?"

No way! Not another lecture. I had done nothing wrong and I couldn't cope with this right now. No way! I
really didn't want to say anything to him.

"Okay," I sniffed back.

Facing away from the now open door, I wiped away my dried tears on the pillow. Why not look presentable at
least? I then turned to him, still lying on my bed. He walked across the room and perched on the side of my
bed.

"Ifiok, I'm really sorry. I was wrong to send you to your room."

For a moment there was a pause, a silence that stretched out until the magnitude of what was spoken landed.

What! His words smashed through the walls I had built in my heart. I looked at him, bewildered, confused and
shocked.

And then he said something that rocked my world. It's probably the greatest lesson that I've ever learned.

"Will you forgive me?"

I wept.

He was there humbling himself to a 7 year old child. And I couldn't hold it in any longer. All the bitterness,
swept away and I wept.

Right then I loved him more intensely than I've ever loved anyone.

"Yeah of course." I replied through uncontrollable sobs.

He hugged me. Then smiled when he saw how much it meant to me. "I love you Ifiok."

I'm not sure how long we were there for, but as he left the room I continued to sob. I was loved. And just as
importantly I had been shown something that impacted my life greatly.

I had learned that mistakes happen, and at that moment it was always best to be humble and seek forgiveness.
There is no perfect person and we all make wrong decisions, or have wrong reactions. But even in the midst of
these imperfections we can humble ourselves and receive great reconciliation.

I wish I could say that that from that point onward I behaved perfectly, never getting into trouble. But that
would be a lie. A tempting lie :-). I got in a lot of trouble after that

In reality, it gave me a profound respect and deep love for my dad. I could trust him even when he was wrong
because he'd always work to make things right. He still speaks and advises me to this day.

I'm coming to the end of this post, and I'm not quite sure what to write next. I could go in several different
directions, but none of them feel appropriate. So maybe those thoughts are for another day. Another blog post.

But before I sign off, let's get back to the story.

I stayed in my room for a while.

My happy contemplation was interrupted by the sounds of happy voices coming from downstairs. My younger
siblings were laughing while watching Lion King for probably the 10th time that week.

I sniffed. Cleared my throat. Wiped my tears. Then I ran to a nearby mirror to hurriedly shake myself together
and rub the redness out of my eyes. All before making the most feeble attempt at a lion's roar as I rushed
downstairs to take my seat amongst my family.

I was a child again.

I was loved.
